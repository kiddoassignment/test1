Intermediate Level

The Intermediate Level course is designed for those who have a basic understanding of music theory and wish to expand their knowledge and skills. It covers essential topics such as:

  • Advanced musical notation:
    Dive deeper into reading complex scores, including articulations, dynamics, and non-standard notation.
  • Harmony and chord progressions:
    Learn to analyze and construct chords, including triads and seventh chords, and explore common chord progressions used in various musical styles.
  • Scales and modes:
    Understand the construction and application of major and minor scales, along with modes such as Dorian, Phrygian, and Mixolydian.
  • Counterpoint:
    Introduce the fundamentals of counterpoint, learning to compose simple two-voice melodies that harmonize effectively.
  • Music form and structure:
    Analyze different musical forms, such as binary and ternary forms, and learn how to structure compositions with clear sections.
  • Ear training and sight-singing:
    Develop the ability to recognize intervals, rhythms, and chords by ear, along with practicing sight-singing skills.

This course builds on the skills learned in the Basic Level, providing a comprehensive pathway to advanced music theory and practice.
